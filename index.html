1. Overview of APCM Services

Advanced Primary Care Management (APCM) services represent a strategic shift towards proactive, patient-centered primary care for Medicare beneficiaries. APCM bundles several care management services—including chronic care, principal care, transitional care, and virtual check-ins—into a single monthly HCPCS code. This streamlined approach aims to improve care access, enhance coordination, and ultimately drive better patient outcomes by focusing on comprehensive, continuous care relationships.

2. APCM HCPCS Codes and Reimbursement

HCPCS Code Description Patient Criteria 2025 National Rate
G0556 APCM for patients with 0-1 chronic condition One or fewer chronic conditions. $15.20
G0557 APCM for patients with ≥2 chronic conditions Conditions expected ≥12 months (or until death) with significant risk. $48.84
G0558 APCM for QMB patients with ≥2 chronic conditions Meets G0557 criteria and is a Qualified Medicare Beneficiary (QMB). $107.07

3. Billing Requirements

To bill an APCM code, practices must have the capability—and document the availability—of each service element below as clinically appropriate for their patient population.

Patient Consent

Obtain and document patient consent (written or verbal).

Initiating Visit

Conduct for new patients or those unseen in ≥3 years.

24/7 Access

Provide urgent access and real-time EHR availability.

Care Continuity

Ensure appointment continuity with a designated team member.

Alternative Care Delivery

Offer home visits, extended hours, or other alternatives.

Comprehensive Management

Assessments, preventive care, medication reconciliation.

Community Coordination

Coordinate community services and document psychosocial goals.

Patient-Centered Care Plan

Maintain and share an electronic care plan with patients.

Population Health

Analyze data to close care gaps and risk-stratify patients.

Performance Measurement

Measure and report on quality, cost, and meaningful EHR use.

4. Eligible Providers

  • Physicians, Nurse Practitioners (NPs), Physician Assistants (PAs), and Clinical Nurse Specialists (CNSs) may bill.
  • Any specialty can bill, though it's designed for primary care.
  • Auxiliary staff may deliver services under "incident to" general supervision.
  • Only one clinician may bill APCM for a patient per calendar month.

5. Frequency & Code Selection

  • Bill once per patient per calendar month.
  • Do not bill APCM in the same month as CCM, PCM, or TCM.
  • Patients can alternate between care management codes month-to-month.
  • Billing APCM attests that all requirements are met and available.

6. Reimbursement & Cost-Sharing

Medicare reimburses based on the Physician Fee Schedule (PFS) national rates. Standard patient cost-sharing (deductible, copay, coinsurance) applies.

QMB Exception: Patients who are Qualified Medicare Beneficiaries (QMB) have no cost-sharing liability. Practices must notify all other patients of potential out-of-pocket costs during consent.

7. Documentation & Attestation

Thorough documentation is critical. Retain records of consent, initiating visits, care plan activities, communications, and performance metrics.

Attestation: Claiming an APCM code serves as your attestation that all service elements are available and were delivered as needed.

8. Implementation Tips

  • ✓ Automate eligibility, consent tracking, and billing in your EHR.
  • ✓ Use standardized consent forms and care plan templates.
  • ✓ Train staff on 24/7 access protocols and asynchronous communication tools.
  • ✓ Perform monthly audits to ensure documentation of all service elements.
  • ✓ Leverage RPM and telehealth alongside APCM to boost engagement.

9. Sample Patient Consent Form

Advanced Primary Care Management (APCM) Consent

(Adapted from AAFP Chronic Care Management Toolkit)

I agree to participate in Advanced Primary Care Management (APCM) services. I understand:

  • APCM services include care-plan development, coordination, and 24/7 access.
  • My clinician and care team will share an electronic care plan with me.
  • These services will be billed to Medicare; I may incur usual cost-sharing unless I am a QMB.
  • I may withdraw consent at any time by notifying my clinician.
